About the Artist

Dan Brown (1949-2022) was a Connecticut-based American painter renowned for his mastery of trompe l'oeil ("fool the eye") painting. His work stands in the tradition of American masters like William Michael Harnett and John Frederick Peto, creating hyperrealistic still lifes that challenge the viewer's perception of reality.

Born in Syracuse, New York, Brown studied at the Paier College of Art in Hamden, Connecticut, where he trained under renowned American realists Ken Davies and Peter Poskas. His subjects ranged from currency paintings in the tradition of Otis Kaye, to vintage postcards, cottage window scenes, and intimate still lifes of everyday objects.

Beyond his fine art career, Brown had a distinguished 25+ year career as a commercial illustrator, creating work for clients including Rolling Stone Magazine, Harlequin Books, Smithsonian, National Geographic, Reader's Digest, Topps, and Disney. His portrait of Bob Dylan for Rolling Stone's "100 Greatest Artists" series exemplifies his technical mastery and artistic vision.

What is a Catalogue Raisonné?

A catalogue raisonné (French for "reasoned catalog") is a comprehensive, scholarly catalog of all known works by an artist. It serves as the definitive reference for authenticating, studying, and understanding an artist's complete body of work.

This digital catalogue raisonné aims to document Dan Brown's artistic legacy, encompassing both his fine art paintings and his extensive commercial illustration work.

Purpose of This Project

This catalogue raisonné was created as a labor of love to preserve and document Dan Brown's artistic legacy. It serves multiple purposes:

  • • Preservation: Creating a permanent record of known works before they are scattered or lost
  • • Discovery: Actively monitoring auction houses and galleries to locate additional works
  • • Authentication: Distinguishing genuine Dan Brown works from those by other artists with the same name
  • • Research: Gathering verified metadata from institutions, galleries, and archives
